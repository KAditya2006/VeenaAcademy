# Veena Academy Phase 3 UX Architecture and Wireframe Blueprint

## 1. Executive UX Direction

Veena Academy should feel like a premium education product, not a conventional coaching institute brochure. The site must quickly answer the emotional and practical questions of parents and students:

- Parents need trust, safety, faculty credibility, results, structure, and accountability.
- Students need confidence, exam relevance, motivation, doubt support, and proof that success feels possible.
- Returning visitors need fast access to admissions, contact, course details, results, and future portal entry points.

The recommended experience is a hybrid model:

- A highly conversion-focused homepage that tells the complete brand story.
- Dedicated pages for high-intent topics like courses, results, faculty, facilities, scholarship, admission, blog, and contact.
- Future-ready portal navigation that can later support Student Portal, Parent Portal, Faculty Portal, Admin Dashboard, LMS, tests, attendance, assignments, and fee management.

## 3. User Journey Maps

### Student Journey

Landing point:
- Usually homepage, course page, results page, or direct social media link.

Reading order:
1. Hero promise
2. Course badges
3. Results or topper proof
4. Course details
5. Faculty and doubt support
6. Gallery or classroom visuals
7. Demo class CTA

Decision points:
- Does this academy help with my exam?
- Are teachers strong?
- Will I get tests and doubts?
- Can I see students like me succeeding?

Trust triggers:
- Topper cards
- Weekly test system
- Doubt sessions
- Course-specific result proof
- Student testimonials

CTA locations:
- Hero: Book Free Demo, WhatsApp
- Courses: Know More
- Results: Talk to Counsellor
- FAQ: Ask a Question
- Contact: Submit Enquiry

Final conversion:
- Book demo class or WhatsApp admission team.

### Parent Journey

Landing point:
- Homepage, results page, about page, faculty page, or contact page.

Reading order:
1. Hero trust promise
2. Student count and faculty credibility
3. Why Choose Us
4. Results and testimonials
5. Facilities and safety
6. Admission process
7. Contact details

Decision points:
- Is this institution credible?
- Will my child be monitored?
- Are teachers experienced?
- Is the environment safe?
- Can I speak to someone easily?

Trust triggers:
- Faculty qualifications
- Parent reviews
- Safe learning environment
- Library and transport
- Counselling process
- Transparent admission steps

CTA locations:
- Hero: Book Free Demo
- Trust section: Speak to Counsellor
- Faculty: Meet Our Faculty
- Admission process: Start Admission Enquiry
- Contact: Call Now

Final conversion:
- Phone call, counselling request, or demo class booking.

### Returning Visitor Journey

Landing point:
- Direct URL, Google search, saved link, or WhatsApp shared URL.

Reading order:
1. Navbar shortcut
2. Contact or course page
3. Admission process
4. Form or call action

Decision points:
- Where is the phone number?
- How do I book demo?
- Which batch is relevant?
- Is admission still open?

Trust triggers:
- Sticky CTA
- Clear contact cards
- Persistent WhatsApp and call buttons
- Admissions Open notice

Final conversion:
- Quick call, WhatsApp, or enquiry form.

### Mobile User Journey

Landing point:
- Homepage or WhatsApp/social link.

Reading order:

Purpose:
- Establish brand promise, admission urgency, and immediate conversion path.

- CTAs: Book Free Demo, Call Now, WhatsApp
- Course chips: JEE, NEET, CUET, Boards, Foundation

- Desktop: two-column layout, copy left, premium classroom/student visual right.
- Mobile: copy first, CTAs stacked, visual below or reduced.

CTA:

- Visual card softly scales in.

## 8. CTA Strategy

### CTA Types and Purpose

Book Free Demo:
- Use when user is evaluating fit.
- Best in hero, navbar, course cards, CTA bands, contact section.

Apply Now:
- Use on admission or scholarship pages where user intent is high.

Download Brochure:
- Use on course detail pages and parent-focused sections.
- Good for users not ready to speak yet.

Call Now:
- Use for urgent, high-trust conversion.
- Best in hero, sticky actions, contact, mobile bottom bar.

WhatsApp:
- Use for low-friction enquiry.
- Best floating action and mobile sticky bar.

Admission Enquiry:
- Use after process, FAQ, and contact sections.

Scholarship Test:
- Use on homepage only if scholarship campaign is active.
- Best as announcement bar or dedicated scholarship section.

### CTA Placement Logic

Hero:
- User is curious. Offer demo, call, WhatsApp.

After Trust/Courses:
- User understands relevance. Offer counselling/demo.

After Results:
- User sees proof. Offer demo or course enquiry.

After Faculty/Facilities:
- Parent trust is stronger. Offer campus visit or call.

After FAQ:
- Objections are reduced. Offer enquiry form.

Contact:
- User is ready. Offer final conversion actions.

## 10. Mobile UX Plan

Thumb-friendly navigation:
- Hamburger target at least 44px.
- Sticky bottom action bar with Call, WhatsApp, Demo.
- Keep primary CTA in first viewport.

Responsive cards:
- Single-column on mobile.
- Use consistent card padding.
- Avoid tiny multi-column text-heavy layouts.

One-hand usability:
- Important CTAs near bottom and center.
- Keep form fields large and easy to tap.

Scroll behavior:
- Short sections.
- Strong headings.
- CTA after key proof sections.

Touch targets:
- Buttons at least 44px high.
- Inputs at least 44px high.
- Accordion headers at least 48px high.

Performance-first:
- Lazy load gallery images.
- Avoid heavy animation on mobile.
- Use reduced-motion support.

## 11. Micro Interaction Recommendations

Buttons:
- Slight lift and soft glow on hover.
- Press state should scale down very slightly.
- Disabled state should reduce opacity and remove lift.

Cards:
- Subtle lift on desktop hover.
- No hover dependency for mobile.

Images:
- Gentle zoom on gallery hover.
- Lazy load images.

Statistics:
- Count up only once when visible.
- Do not loop.

Navigation:
- Sticky nav gains glass background after scroll.
- Active section indicator should be subtle.

Forms:
- Focus ring must be visible.
- Show success/error states near field.
- Loading state on submit.

Gallery:
- Hover zoom and title overlay.
- Tap opens future lightbox.

Testimonials:
- Slider should pause on hover and be swipe-friendly.

## 12. Accessibility Plan

Keyboard:
- All buttons, links, accordions, and form controls keyboard accessible.
- Visible focus states.

Screen readers:
- Semantic header, nav, main, section, footer.
- Proper labels for form fields.
- Accordion buttons with aria-expanded.

Color contrast:
- Maintain AA contrast for text and controls.
- Avoid orange text on white for small text unless contrast passes.

Typography:
- Body text minimum 16px.
- Line height 1.6 to 1.8.
- Avoid dense paragraphs.

Touch accessibility:
- Minimum 44px tap targets.
- Sticky actions should not cover content.

Reduced motion:
- Respect prefers-reduced-motion.
- Use transforms and opacity rather than layout-shifting animation.

## 15. UX Best Practices

- Place the strongest trust proof before asking for deep commitment.
- Keep course cards scannable and action-oriented.
- Use real photos wherever possible.
- Keep copy parent-friendly and student-friendly.
- Repeat CTAs only when mindset changes.
- Use progressive disclosure for complex course information.
- Keep forms short on homepage.
- Reserve detailed forms for admission pages.
- Make phone and WhatsApp always easy to access.
- Maintain consistent spacing and card structure.

## 16. Potential UX Mistakes to Avoid

- Putting every detail on the homepage without hierarchy.
- Overusing animation or glass effects.
- Using generic stock photos that do not build trust.
- Hiding phone number or WhatsApp behind menus.
- Making course cards too text-heavy.
- Showing results without context.
- Using too many CTAs in one section.
- Making mobile users scroll too far before contact actions.
- Adding a mega menu before enough pages exist.
- Ignoring parent-specific trust content.
- Using low-contrast orange text for important small copy.
- Creating future portals with a different visual language.
